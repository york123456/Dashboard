Have Americans’ wages kept up with inflation? That depends

 

A majority of Americans see inflation as a very big problem for the country today. How well U.S. wages have kept pace with rising prices depends on which measure you use: Based on four different gauges of inflation, the buying power of the median wage has grown anywhere from 11% to 22% since the turn of the century.

Key findings about Americans and mental health

 

Americans today are nearly as likely to say they put a lot of effort into taking care of their mental health as they are to say this about their physical health. Though most U.S. adults rate their mental health positively, around one-in-five do not.

 
 

5 facts about Africa’s population growth

 

While global population growth is projected to slow over the rest of the century, Africa stands out for its relatively young and increasing population. Today, about one-in-three people under 25 live in Africa – and that share will be close to half by 2100, according to projections from the United Nations.
