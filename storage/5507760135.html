The S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite set new intraday records Tuesday, as Micron Technology led chip shares higher. Meanwhile, oil futures were mixed, with global benchmark Brent crude once again surpassing $100 a barrel after the U.S. attacked Iranian ships in what Washington claimed was a defensive maneuver.
