The Pretext Behind the Trump Administration Labeling Cuba a State Sponsor of Terrorism

On May 21, Secretary of State Marco Rubio stood at the Miami Homestead Airport and told reporters that Cuba “has been one of the leading sponsors of terrorism in the entire region.” He paired that with a familiar warning: “Having a failed state 90 miles from our shores run by friends of our adversaries poses a threat to the national security of the United States.” The remarks landed the same day the Justice Department unsealed a murder indictment against former Cuban President Raul Castro, and the same day United States Southern Command welcomed the USS Nimitz carrier strike group to the Caribbean.

The Search for the Missing in Syria: Learning from the Past

Syria faces one of the largest missing persons crises in the world. Estimates from the Syrian government suggest that between 130,000 and 300,000 individuals have been disappeared over nearly five decades, the majority since 2011, in a country of approximately 23 million people. This places Syria among the most affected contexts globally, both in absolute and relative terms. Nearly every Syrian family has been affected by disappearances. Addressing this crisis is therefore not only a humanitarian imperative, but also central to any meaningful process of truth, justice, stabilization, peacebuilding, and recovery.

Anti-Weaponization Fund
Floyd v. Department of Justice (E.D. Va.) 
Case No. 1:26-cv-01399

May 29, 2026: Judge Brinkema enjoined Defendants from taking any action related to the creation or operation of the Anti-Weaponization Fund, including transferring money to the Fund, considering submitted claims, or disbursing any funds. Judge Brinkema also denied the Plaintiffs’ motion for expedited briefing and granted the Defendants’ request for additional time.

 

Government Facilities, Property, and Security Modifications
Beatty v. Trump (D.D.C.)
Case No. 1:25-cv-04480

May 29, 2026: Judge Cooper issued an opinion and order granting Rep. Beatty’s motion for partial summary judgment, declaring that the Board’s renaming of the Center the “Donald J. Trump and John F. Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts” violated the Center’s organic statute and permanently enjoined Defendants from naming the Center for anyone other than President Kennedy absent an Act of Congress. The court also sua sponte granted summary judgment to Beatty on her voting-rights claim, holding that the May 2025 bylaw amendment stripping ex officio trustees of the right to vote is void, and denied Defendants’ cross-motion as to those claims. Finally, the court granted in part Beatty’s motion for a preliminary injunction, finding her likely to succeed in showing that the Board breached its fiduciary duty of prudence in voting to close the Center, and enjoined Defendants from implementing the March 16 closure vote, though the injunction permits the OBBBA-funded capital repairs to proceed and does not bar the Board from voting to close anew after prudent deliberation. 

 

Government Facilities, Property, and Security Modifications
DC Preservation League v. Board of Trustees of the Kennedy Center (D.D.C.) 
Case No. 1:26-cv-00981

May 29, 2026: Judge Cooper denied Plaintiffs’ motion for a preliminary injunction, concluding that DC Preservation League had not demonstrated a likelihood of success on its APA, ultra vires, or mandamus claims challenging the Kennedy Center’s planned renovation. Judge Cooper noted that the Plaintiffs did not carry their burden for preliminary relief, but their challenges may be revisited if the scope of construction changes.
