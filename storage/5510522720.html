Too good for too long? Arms control, foreign policy, and the American people in 2026

The global order is shifting, and the US is driving the change. Is this change a natural progression, or the singular worldview of President Trump? Does the American public largely agree with where we are headed, or is it their disconnect with policy that is allowing these changes? Why Congress and senior officials must deny Trump a 'nuclear escape' in Iran

Former assistant secretary Mallory Stewart on informal limits, behavioral arms control, and risk reduction

Mallory Stewart, former United States assistant secretary for arms control, speaks with Bulletin nuclear editor François Diaz-Maurin on what mechanisms of risk reduction can maintain strategic stability in a multipolar world. Stewart describes the challenges posed by new threats from emerging technologies and how much the lost expertise in the US administration may affect the capacity of the US to design and develop treaties. This Bulletin magazine article is available to all readers for a limited time.
