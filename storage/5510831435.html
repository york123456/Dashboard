Right-wing populism in the decade since Brexit

 

The results of the Brexit vote in 2016 were a shock to many in the U.K. and elsewhere, and right-wing populism has gained influence and support across Europe in the decade since. Our new data essay highlights factors that have shaped the rise of right-wing populism, using Center survey data from 2022 to 2026.

  • In several European countries, EU gets more positive ratings today than during Brexit vote

 
 

Americans have become less confident in Trump’s decision-making on Ukraine

 

Americans are becoming less confident that President Donald Trump can make good decisions about the war between Russia and Ukraine. About a third (32%) are very or somewhat confident he can make good decisions on this issue, down from 40% in August 2025. Confidence has dropped among Republicans and Democrats alike since last year.

Americans remain critical of Trump administration’s approach to Iran

 

Months into the U.S. military conflict with Iran, Americans continue to express significant doubts about how Trump and his administration are handling the situation. About six-in-ten (62%) disapprove of Trump’s handling of the military action against Iran, including 45% who strongly disapprove. This compares with 36% who approve.
