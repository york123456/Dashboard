1. European Workers See Jobs, but Not Workforce Engagement

The Data: Fifty-seven percent of European employees in 2025 said it was a good time to find a job in their local area, matching the previous record high the year before.

The Engagement Gap: At the same time, employee engagement in Europe remained low at 12%, among the lowest regional levels in the world.

2. Thailand Leads Southeast Asia in Engagement Gains

The Data: Thirty-four percent of Thai employees were engaged in 2025, more than double the 15% engaged in 2014 and one of the highest engagement levels in Southeast Asia.

Regional Context: No other country in Southeast Asia has seen a bigger increase in the same period. Thailand's 19-percentage-point increase since 2014 is also one of the top five largest increases anywhere in the world.

Read More

3. India's Employee Engagement Falls to 23%

The Data: Twenty-three percent of employees in India were engaged in 2025, the lowest for that country in four years.

The Trend: Engagement remains above the global average of 20%, but it has fallen from recent highs, while the percentage of Indian employees who are not engaged has risen to 59%.

4. Americans Oppose Local AI Data Centers

The Data: Seventy-one percent of Americans oppose the construction of an AI data center in their local area, including 48% who strongly oppose it.

Local Comparison: Opposition to AI data centers exceeds opposition to building a local nuclear energy plant — which, according to the same March survey, 53% oppose, including 34% strongly.

5. The “Pay Later” Credit Card Worry Link

The Data: Twenty-seven percent of U.S. adults report that they frequently or occasionally use installment plans when making online purchases.

Financial Stress: At 57%, installment-plan use is highest among adults who are very worried about making minimum credit card payments, compared with 11% using installment plans among those who are not worried at all about their card payments.
