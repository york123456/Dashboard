Politics from the pulpit

 

Two-thirds of U.S. adults who regularly attend religious services say they have heard their clergy speak about at least one political or social issue in the past few months, according to a new Pew Research Center survey. Of the seven topics we asked about, abortion, Israel and homosexuality were among the most commonly cited.

On these topics, regular attenders report hearing more from their clergy about:

  • Opposing abortion (30%) than supporting abortion rights (2%)
  • Opposing homosexuality (20%) than encouraging acceptance of people who are gay or lesbian (3%)
  • Supporting Israel (15%) than criticizing it (3%)
Our survey also asked regular attenders if they’ve heard their clergy speak about immigration, the environment, transgender identity and U.S. military action in Iran. Read more about the messages Americans report hearing from their clergy and how they differ by religious group.
