Want to find some of the most interesting and best writing on Medium? Go to the publications. Publications are the backbone of Medium. They’re the ones who do the heavy work of reading, editing, and curating some of the most interesting writing on the site.

For this issue of the Medium Newsletter, rather than covering a topic or specific piece, we want to highlight some of the publications that make those pieces possible in the first place.

···

Interested in culture writing?

Take a tour of these publications: Counter Arts features writing from the broader cultural world; Books Are Our Superpower for books; Frame Rated and Cinemania for film; The Riff and Rock n’ Heavy for music; Tastyble and The Rooted for food; Full Frame and Live View for photography. And if you want to just read conversations about general pop culture, you can always check out Fanfare or The Ugly Monster.

···

Where to get started

The great thing about publications (the vast majority of which are run and organized by Medium users) is that there are literally thousands of them on just about any topic under the sun. But if you’re having trouble finding them, never fear — finding a great publication is as simple as finding a great story you like. You will always see what publication it is featured in directly above its title, right by the writer’s name.

Featuring The Most Annoying Rule of English Grammar That We Put Up With by William Sidnam in Language Lab.

There are a few more places you can go to find great stories and publications. First, log in and check out your home page. These individual picks are determined by what you and the people you follow read and connect with. You can control what you see in your home feed by interacting with the stories you like and want to see more of through signals you send to us — so always be sure to clap, highlight, comment, and repost.

Second, you can find them by exploring specific topics. Head to the Explore Topics page and click on any of the specific topic pages. This will bring you to a list of recommended stories, which can help you discover publications related to those topics! (Conversely, if you do not want to see stories recommended to you about specific topics, you can now mute topics.)

The Dating tag, featuring The Gender War Within Gen Z Has the Wrong Enemy by Martina H in Fourth Wave and I Tried A Queer Speed Dating Event by Eleni Stephanides in Prism & Pen.

The Medium staff also maintains a massive list of publications that are currently accepting submissions — a good way to find publications that are currently active. (And if you want to submit something you’ve written to a publication, check out this guide from The Medium Handbook by Zulie @ Medium so you can set yourself up for success.)

Once you’ve found your new favorite publication, make sure to follow it so you can find it easily from your home page!

···

Do you want to read deep, moving creative nonfiction?

The Memorist, as its name suggests, focuses almost entirely on memoirs. Age of Empathy publishes monthly prompts that will help you understand other points of view. Frazzled and The Parenting Portal feature writing aimed at those raising kids, while Life Without Children has essays for those who choose to be child-free. For women who are approaching middle age, there’s Middle Pause. The Grief Book Club shares essays and opinions on the grieving process. Fourth Wave, Bitchy, Modern Women, and The Virago have writings from a feminist point of view, while Prism Pen elevates voices from the LGBTQ+ community.

Celebrating editors

None of these publications would be anything without their editors. That’s why we here at Medium want to reward these unsung heroes of the writing process.

The newly announced Editor Partner Program makes sure we can reward editors at qualifying publications for the work they do. Here’s how it works:

“Once a publication is in the program, editors will be able to claim stories in the submissions queue to indicate they’re working on them. When the story is published, the editor will earn 25% of what the story earned.

Writers won’t see any drop in their story earnings. For example, if a writer’s story in a publication would earn $100, the writer still will earn that amount — the editor will get a 25% bonus on top of that. We’ve increased the total amount of Medium subscriber funds we pay out to our community to make this program happen.”

We’ll be opening up the Editor Partner Program for a small group of publications in June, with a sitewide launch coming later in the summer. Make sure to follow the Medium Blog (also a publication, by the way!) for the latest updates.

Want to learn something new?

The Academic is a publication that publishes science papers, or get more focused with others like Microbial Instincts (microbiology), Fossil et. al (paleontology), or The Environment (environmental science and climate). Tech-focused publications like AI Advances, Code like a Girl, and Mac O’Clock produce some of the most-read stories on Medium. Curious about the origins of words or how we communicate? Go to Language Lab. Interested in authentic Japanese culture? Read Japonica. For news, politics, and social justice, check out, Momentum, Policy Panorama, or An Injustice!

···

A final thought

This week we were struck by How To Quit Everything and Make Stained Glass from Poor Steward, with Katlyn Roberts. This story, published in mental health publication Invisible Illness, is a wonderful perspective from someone who feels different but still feels the urge to “create something beautiful.”

One thing to keep in mind, as sometimes we fight the urge to have everything perfect before we create:

Spiders weave their webs anywhere, right? They’re pretty famous for it. Whatever sticks, sticks, whether they mean for it to or not. Sometimes, this is what sustains them. Sometimes, it weighs them down. But they get to just focus on the weaving — on the obsessive, monotropic, locked-in weaving.

Weave on, writers and editors.
