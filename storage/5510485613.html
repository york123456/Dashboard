“When I first lit the Amnesty candle, I had in mind the old Chinese proverb: Better light a candle than curse the darkness.”

 

British lawyer Peter Benenson reflects on founding Amnesty International, the human rights organization he launched in 1961, after reading about two Portuguese students imprisoned for toasting freedom under the Salazar dictatorship. On this day in 1961, Benenson published “The Forgotten Prisoners” in The Observer, laying the foundation for what later became Amnesty International.
