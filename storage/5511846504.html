Book Smarts or Street Smarts?

The CMT Program ensures you have both, giving you a competitive edge in your career.

"Book Smarts" vs. "Street Smarts"


We've all known someone, or perhaps you ARE this someone, that's "book smart," that can study and absorb information, memorize facts and formulas, and learn concepts academically. 


We've also known people that are "street smart," who excel at real-world integration and application of concepts.


No matter what sector you work in, you've got to prove you've got BOTH to stand out

Employers are looking for individuals who have proven that they not only understand technical analysis concepts from an academic perspective, but that they can apply those same concepts in real-world scenarios.


That's why the CMT Program develops and tests a candidate's broad knowledge of technical analysis, as well as their ability to apply that knowledge in situations they might encounter during their career.


Earning the CMT designation is proof that you've mastered a wide spectrum of technical analysis concepts, not just memorized enough of the curriculum to pass a test.

How can the CMT Program help candidates prove mastery of technical analysis?


The CMT Program consists of three exams, with each subsequent level building on the skills and knowledge gained in the last.


The first two exams are multiple choice, and they test the candidate's understanding of the concepts covered in the curriculum. The third exam is a combination of multiple choice and short answer essay questions, and it test's the candidate's ability to integrate what they've learned.

Level I


The CMT Level I Exam focuses on the basic knowledge of the terminology and analytical tools used in technical analysis.

Level II


The CMT Level II Exam measures the candidate’s competency in the application of concepts, theory, and techniques covered by the required readings.

Level III


The CMT Level III Exam tests the candidate’s ability to integrate his or her understanding of the concepts identified in Level I with the practical application learned in Level II.

Once you've completed the CMT Program, it's evidence that your skills are at another level compared to your peers, giving you the edge you need in such a competitive industry.
