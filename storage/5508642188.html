﻿How creative thinking can move you forward

Welcome to “The McKinsey Publishing Guide to getting unstuck.” In this first email, we’ll explore how companies can break through stagnant thinking and drive innovation. A key approach? Cultivating a more inclusive environment. We’ll share practical strategies, including fostering empathy and embracing an artistic perspective, to unlock new levels of creativity and improve performance.

While “thinking outside the box” may be a common phrase, its core message—the need for unconventional problem-solving—remains crucial. But moving beyond the cliché, how can organizations practically cultivate creativity?

Corporate leaders often have a reputation for being rigid, but McKinsey’s Brooke Weddle and coauthors write that transforming workplace culture﻿ to be more inclusive can serve as a significant driver of innovation. By fostering an environment where diverse perspectives are valued and integrated, organizations can unlock a wealth of creative potential, thereby enhancing performance and productivity. 

Read on to learn more about the various ways organizations can foster creative thinking through encouraging empathy, tapping into an artistic mindset, and more.

1. Prioritize building a culture of psychological safety﻿

﻿When people feel safe to express ideas without fear of judgment, they are more likely to contribute innovative solutions. This creates a culture where learning from failures is valued, promoting resilience and continuous improvement in both work and personal endeavors.

Read: Five bold moves to quickly transform your organization’s culture﻿

2. Adopt an artist’s mindset﻿

﻿Reframing challenges as creative projects allows you to approach them with a sense of curiosity and playfulness. This can lead to innovative solutions and a more fulfilling experience, whether you’re tackling a complex work project or navigating a personal dilemma.

Read: Author Talks: Can thinking like an artist improve your decisions?

3. Disrupt familiar patterns﻿

﻿When routines become entrenched, both work and life can feel stagnant. Introducing novel experiences—whether a new hobby, a different work approach, or exploring unfamiliar environments—can stimulate fresh thinking and unlock solutions previously unseen.

Read: Sparking creativity in teams: An executive’s guide﻿

4. Cultivate empathy to unlock collaborative solutions﻿

﻿By actively listening and seeking to understand others’ perspectives, you can break down communication barriers and foster a more inclusive environment. This not only enhances teamwork at work but also enriches personal relationships, leading to more fulfilling interactions and a sense of shared purpose.

Read: It’s cool to be kind: The value of empathy at work﻿

5. Embrace the unpredictability of the creative process﻿

﻿Recognizing that great ideas often emerge from unexpected places can free you from the pressure of perfection. This mindset shift empowers you to experiment, take calculated risks, and see setbacks as opportunities for learning and growth, both professionally and personally.

Read: ‘The creative process is fabulously unpredictable. A great idea cannot be predicted’
